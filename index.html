Impact of Electric Vehicles on Power Grids of Developed and Developing Nations

Introduction

Electricity generation and consumption plays a major role in the economic status of a nation. With the growing need of minimizing the fuel consumption, which has a detrimental effect on the environment, a shift is considered to Electric Vehicles. But is this a silver bullet for all the hitches? In this study we are examining the grid infrastructure of countries with varied economic statuses. With the data for existing consumption and generation, fleet data and fuel consumption, we create a basic model without the presence of Electric Vehicles. We then estimate the introduction of EVs in these grid systems and evaluate how they will impact the electric grid. EVs will have different impact in different regions. In some cases, we can fill up the duck curve and stabilize the demand, while in some cases EVs will have a negative impact on the grid.

Duck Curve

The duck curve named by its resemblance to a duck is a graph of power production over the course of a day that shows the timing imbalance between peak demand and renewable energy production. In most cases a dip is seen during the off-peak period that is during the morning-afternoon hours and a steep hike is noticed at the peak hours from 6pm to 9pm as shown in Figure 3. At the peak hours, a steep ramp is required that causes a stress on the grid and a risk of overgeneration. We have gathered the data for the hourly consumption of electricity (from The Independent Electricity System Operator-IESO) during the day for Canada and relate it to this curve and see how the EVs will impact this graph.
